01 — About Project

What This
Dashboard Does

This project presents a comprehensive visual analysis of the IMDb Top 1000 Movies dataset, transforming raw data into a rich, interactive storytelling experience.

Built using Tableau Public, the dashboard explores relationships between ratings, genres, release years, gross revenue, and runtime — uncovering patterns hidden in decades of cinematic history.

Whether you're a film enthusiast, data analyst, or researcher, this dashboard provides an intuitive lens into what makes a movie truly great.

07 — Key Findings

Data
Insights

Patterns and revelations drawn from a century of cinema — visualized through the lens of IMDb's top-rated films.

01
Genre Trends
🎭
Drama #1
Drama Dominates the Top Charts
Drama accounts for over 35% of all Top 1000 entries, consistently producing the highest-rated films across every decade. Action and Thriller trail behind, showing strong growth post-2000.
02
IMDb Ratings
⭐
8.1avg
Average Rating Holds Steady at 8.1
Films in the Top 1000 maintain a tight rating band between 7.6 and 9.3. The 1990s–2000s era shows the highest concentration of 8.5+ rated movies, indicating a golden age for acclaimed cinema.
03
Box Office
💰
$300M+
High Ratings ≠ High Revenue
Critically acclaimed films often underperform commercially. The highest-grossing movies (Adventure, Sci-Fi) rarely occupy the top rating positions — revealing a clear divide between critic favorites and audience blockbusters.
04
Runtime
⏱️
127min
Longer Films Tend to Score Higher
Movies with runtimes between 120–180 minutes average significantly higher IMDb ratings than shorter films. This suggests that epic, immersive narratives resonate more strongly with both critics and audiences alike.
